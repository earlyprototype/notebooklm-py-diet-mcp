# Slide Design Templates

Three ready-to-use design templates for NotebookLM slide generation.
Copy the contents of any template below into the `instructions` parameter
of `generate_and_download` (or `generate_slide_deck`) to control the
visual style of generated slides.

Templates of 40-80 lines produce the best results. Shorter is better --
every line should earn its place. You can create custom templates by
following the same structure: tone, colours, typography, layout
variations, and prohibitions.

---

## Corporate

Style: Strategy consulting, board presentations, investor decks.

Tone: Authoritative, precise, information-dense, trustworthy.

Colour palette:
  Background: clean white
  Text: sharp black, medium grey for secondary
  Accent: deep royal blue for headers, charts, key data
  Data layering: graduated greys for hierarchy

Typography:
  Headlines: serif font, bold -- conveys editorial authority
  Body and data: clean sans-serif -- conveys analytical precision
  Key metrics: large sans-serif bold, 2-3x body size

Layout variations:
  - Executive summary: thin blue rule at top, 2-column narrative + chart split
  - Data table: full-width, hairline borders, blue column headers, alternating row shading
  - Chart + narrative: 60:40 split, chart left, interpretation right
  - Key metrics dashboard: 3-4 metric cards in a row with large numbers
  - Process flow: 3-5 horizontal stages connected by thin arrows
  - Recommendation: action title at top, numbered list, evidence callout with blue border

Rules:
  - Every slide states a conclusion in the title, not just a topic
  - Strict left-alignment throughout, no centred body text
  - Charts use only blue + grey palette, no decorative colour
  - No illustrations, background images, gradients, drop shadows, or 3D effects
  - No large empty spaces -- information density is the point

## Educational

Style: Workshop facilitation, training sessions, learning materials.

Tone: Warm, approachable, clear, encouraging.

Colour palette:
  Background: soft warm white or pale cream
  Text: dark charcoal, not pure black
  Primary accent: teal or deep green for headings and highlights
  Secondary accent: warm amber or coral for callouts and emphasis

Typography:
  Headlines: rounded sans-serif, bold, generous size
  Body: clean sans-serif with wide line spacing for readability
  Callouts: slightly larger, accent colour, used sparingly

Layout variations:
  - Section opener: large centred title with subtle background shape, section number
  - Concept explanation: heading left, supporting visual or diagram right
  - Key takeaway: accent-coloured panel with large quote or principle statement
  - Step-by-step: numbered sequence with icons or simple illustrations per step
  - Discussion prompt: large question text centred, open space below
  - Comparison: two-column side-by-side with clear labels

Rules:
  - One main idea per slide, never overcrowded
  - Generous whitespace to let content breathe
  - Speaker notes carry the detail, slides carry the headline
  - No dense data tables or complex charts -- simplify into key numbers
  - No dark backgrounds -- keep it bright and inviting

## Creative

Style: Pitch decks, marketing, design portfolios, thought leadership.

Tone: Bold, contemporary, confident, visually striking.

Colour palette:
  Background: alternating dark (near-black) and light (off-white) slides
  Text: high contrast against background
  Accent: single vibrant colour (electric blue, coral, or lime) used sparingly
  Restriction: maximum three colours on any slide

Typography:
  Headlines: large bold sans-serif, impactful, may use mixed case for emphasis
  Body: minimal text, clean sans-serif, generous spacing
  Feature text: oversized pull quotes or single-word statements

Layout variations:
  - Hero statement: single bold sentence centred on dark background
  - Image + caption: full-bleed image with thin text overlay bar
  - Split contrast: half dark / half light with content on each side
  - Grid showcase: 2x2 or 3x3 image grid with minimal captions
  - Data highlight: single large number or statistic with one-line context
  - Closing: bold call-to-action centred, contact details minimal

Rules:
  - Less text is more -- aim for 10-15 words maximum per slide
  - Every slide should work as a standalone visual
  - Use high-quality imagery, no clip art or stock illustrations
  - No bullet point lists -- restructure as visual elements
  - Maintain consistent accent colour throughout, never mix accents
